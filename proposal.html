Prior Authorization Deep Dive

PA is a $98B administrative cost center with only ~3% software penetration — one of the largest services-to-software conversion opportunities in any industry. The procedural/imaging subcategory is the hardest, highest-value, and least-automated segment.

Why PA Is Hard to Automate

Payer Fragmentation

Every payer — UHC, Aetna, Cigna, each of 33+ BCBS plans, Medicaid MCOs — has its own formulary, medical policies, required forms, and submission portal. A knee MRI might require PA from one payer with specific clinical criteria, and be auto-approved by another.

Clinical Judgment Requirement

PA is not pure paperwork. It requires reading the patient's chart, matching clinical evidence to payer-specific medical necessity criteria, extracting the right data points, and writing narrative justification. This is where LLMs matter.

Submission Channel Chaos

Some payers accept electronic (X12 278). Many require proprietary web portals with CAPTCHAs. Others still rely on fax. ~20% of PAs still require a phone call. Architecture must be hybrid: FHIR API + RPA + fax + voice AI.

Cost Per Transaction

Manual PA costs $10.97 per transaction (CAQH 2024). Fully electronic drops to $5.79. At 45 PAs/physician/week across 1.1M physicians, even small per-unit savings compound to billions.

The CMS-0057-F Mandate (Effective Jan 1, 2027)

What it requires: Payers must implement a FHIR R4-based Prior Authorization API that can: (a) identify all covered services requiring PA, (b) specify documentation requirements, (c) support electronic PA request/response exchange, and (d) communicate approval/denial with specific rationale. Decisions within 72 hours (urgent) or 7 calendar days (standard) — halving previous timeframes. Payers must publicly report PA metrics.

Who's covered: Medicare Advantage, state Medicaid/CHIP, Medicaid MCOs, QHP issuers on exchanges. Commercial/employer plans are NOT directly mandated.

Critical signal: 50+ major insurers voluntarily committed in mid-2025 to simplifying PA, targeting 80% real-time electronic PA approvals by 2027.
